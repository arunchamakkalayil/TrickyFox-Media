    Wayanad is a district in the north-eastern part of God’s own country, Kerala. The place is situated on the southern tip of the Deccan Plateau, glorified by the Western Ghats with lofty ridges and clear lakes. The dense forests, lush green jungles, Wayanad tourist places and deep valleys are a must for any traveler.


    Best locations for your elegant photo shoots in Wayanad

    • Meenmutty Waterfalls: For Scenic Beauty. Meenmutty Falls gives a glimpse of natural beauty surrounded by the mountain ranges of Western Ghats.The waterfall is located upriver the Neyyar reservoir area and travellers have to trek through the dense forests to reach the falls

    • Soochipara Falls: For Candid Pictures. Surrounded by deciduous, evergreen forests, Soochipara is among the best waterfalls in the region. The winding roads amidst tea plantations also provide visitors with scenic views of the best tea estates in Wayanad.

    • Chembra Peak: A Heart Shaped Lake. Situated 2100 m above sea level, Chembra is the highest peak in Wayanad district and lives up to its billing of being a picturesque location as the route to the peak is past waterfalls and lush greenery. Chembra has managed to maintain its pristine nature over the years, and many exotic species of rare flora and fauna can be seen here. It is a prime spot for trekking and guided tours are available and necessary to make the climb.

    • Banasura Sagar Dam: Charming View Of Mountain Folds. Banasura Sagar Dam is a picturesque tourist hotspot in Wayanad. Its sprawling reservoir of clear water is surrounded by lush green hills and it’s dotted with tiny islands. Banasura Sagar is a photographer’s delight, and it’s one of the most spellbinding places to visit in Wayanad.

    • Ghat View Point: Enjoy The Sunset. The lofty mountain peaks, the gurgling stream, luxuriant vegetation and the bird’s eye view of the deep valley on the south, with its winding roads, are breathtaking.

    • Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary: Spot The Animals

    • Edakkal Caves: Stunning Vistas Of Nature.The caves are two natural rock formations believed to have been formed by a large split in a huge rock. The carvings inside are extremely beautiful.

    • Bathery Jain Temple: Visit The Ancient Temple.

    • Neelimala View Point: Witness The Wonders Of Nature.

    • Pookot Lake: A Heavenly Escapade

    • Pakshipathalam Bird Sanctuary: Spot The Birds

    • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary: Witness The Wild Side

    • Thirunelli Temple: Witness The Historical Site

    • Kuruva Island: A Lush Green Paradise. Dwelling deep into this island, you can sight many attractive things like bridges made up of bamboo trees and other rare species of trees. The uninhabited island is home to rare species of birds, orchids, herbal plants etc. Coming so much closer to the nature, you might be feeling Kuruva Island as a nature's gift to Wayanad
